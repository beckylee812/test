A company is has purchased some beachfront property that it intends to develop into a resort. However, it is uncertain how much future demand there will be for its resort and it is concerned about overcapitalising. Thus it is unsure whether to build a large resort or a small resort. An engineer suggests to the company that they might build a small resort now and expand it later if the demand is high enough.

The land available is limited so if the company decides to build a small resort now and wishes to obtain the option to expand it later then expansion must be built over the initial stage. This means that the initial stage must be designed to be adaptable so that it can incorporate the expansion if necessary, which will increase its cost.
